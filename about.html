About


Background

African-Americans disproportionately suffer from health issues more than any other population. There is compelling evidence that there is a definite need for the population to be educated, empowered and given the tools to change their health outcomes. It is absolutely necessary that the nation’s health improve. That will require federal, state and local agencies to work with communities at a grass roots level.


Currently, very few organizations are able to penetrate African-American faith-based organizations. The Rochester Health Engagement Partners (RHEP) were able achieve that. RHEP became a trusted resource to the churches in a variety of ways. Community Health Engagement Partners is formerly known as RHEP, the organization from which it evolved. There are some organizations that perform a segment of what Community Health Engagement Partners provide. However, the services and tools provided to educate and empower individuals are limited. Community Engagement Partners will be a total service provider for the African-American faith-based community.

Description

Community Health Engagement Partners is a not-for-profit company (sole proprietor) that makes formal introductions to the African-American faith-based leadership and receives endorsement to:

  • Assess the congregation’s specific health needs via health screenings, surveys and customized topics upon request.
  • Provide training to site coordinators in regards to presenting health information and program implementation.
  • Provide current data and accurate information to distribute to participants.
  • Collaborate with other agencies and programs within the Rochester area in order to receive and deliver information on multiple topics.

Vision

To provide services and resources to diverse communities of all races, interview local experts via television to reach targeted audience.

  • To serve as a consultant to organizations to get their message out to the community.
  • Serve as a resource in multiple areas such as job readiness and education.
  • Collaborate with local and regional areas to become the premier resource service provider in the United States.
  • Provide experts in various fields throughout the United States to educate and empower individuals to obtain greater health outcomes.
  • Utilize media to capture a large audience; talk show that has speakers, healthy cooking; physical activity; etc.

Objectives

  • Streamline Community Health Education and Customer Service to the African-American churches and other groups, clubs, etc.
  • Train individuals/auxiliaries in faith based organizations to customize and facilitate health activities that are desired by their congregations/groups.
  • Effectively communicate community support and resources to the African-American church leadership.
  • Increase the number of churches and other groups that are educating members and the community about health and to lessen the death rates among African-Americans from heart disease, high blood pressure and kidney disease.

Services

  • Quarterly health seminars on topics that affect African-Americans.
  • Annual projects, activities, contests and initiatives that will involve participation from faith based organization, women’s groups, men’s groups and the African-American community.
  • Serve as a resource for identifying speakers on specific health topics.
  • Dissemination of health literature.
  • Provide services via licensed and certified health professionals.

An emphasis will be placed on social, emotional, physical, mental and environmental health inclusive of, but not limited to:

  • Diabetes
  • High Blood Pressure
  • Mental Health
  • Obesity
  • Perinatal Health
  • HIV-AIDS
  • Heart Disease
  • Early Childhood Health
